\section{Executive Summary}

This document presents a comprehensive financial analysis and investment opportunity for Fractal Finance, a no-code personal finance automation platform that acts on users' financial goals and budgeting targets by letting the user define dynamic triggers and criteria to run autonomously on their transaction data. Our three-phase approach prioritizes product validation and market fit before full monetization, with disclosed capital allocation across each phase.

\subsection{Three-Phase Roadmap}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
\item \textbf{Phase 1A (Months 0–6):} Closed beta, product–market-fit validation (no monetisation)
\item \textbf{Phase 1B (Months 7–12):} Public launch and funnel optimisation (no monetisation)
\item \textbf{Phase 2 (Months 13–24):} Subscription roll-out and unit-economics optimisation
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Key Investment Highlights}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
\item \textbf{Market Opportunity:} \$12/month pricing validated with target Gen Z/millennial cohorts
\item \textbf{Capital Efficiency:} \$175,000 funds both Phase 1A and 1B with a 25\% contingency buffer
\item \textbf{Path to 5.8$\times$ LTV:CAC:} Unit-economics model hits 5.8$\times$ by Month 24
\item \textbf{Sticky Retention:} Cross-account automations drive 2.5$\times$ higher annual retention than peers
\item \textbf{Open Banking Tailwind:} Canadian Consumer-Driven Banking Act (2026) cuts data costs 60–80\%
\end{itemize}

\begin{itemize}

\item \textbf{Enhanced Data Quality:} Direct API access improves reliability
\item \textbf{Market Opportunity:} 9 million Canadians currently share credentials insecurely\footnote{Reuters, "What open banking means for Canada's saturated banking market," 2024}
\end{itemize}
