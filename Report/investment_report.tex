\noindent Of course, it is important to contextualize these costs. For instance, although Tiktok/Instagram ads have a higher CPC cost, they also offer detailed analytics, guaranteed traction, and a hyper-targeted reach. This premium is similarly justified for university partnerships by the clustered nature of these environments, where trust is high and word of mouth. In addition, The ability to be present enables real-time engagement, and promotes organic growth. \\\\ These figures (other than referrals) also assume a ~17\% engagement rate between clicks and waitlists/installs. We understand per-click figures for a novel fintech app can be lower than other industries, but we plan to increase these figures over time. Specifics about methodology and channel choice can be discussed with the team. \\

\textbf{CPI/CPC Estimation Methodology:}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{*Reddit Finance:} \$0.50-\$1.50 CPC range for niche subreddits\footnote{Aimers, "How Much Do Reddit Ads Cost in 2025," aimers.io}. We estimate \$1.00 for waitlist signups and \$0.80 for post-launch clicks in highly targeted finance communities like r/personalfinance, r/frugal. However, Reddit is known for unresolved botting problems, so we've halved our expected engagement rate
\item \textbf{**University Partnerships:} Based on \$400-\$2,185 education sector CAC\footnote{UPCEA, "Understanding Cost Per Inquiry in Higher Education Marketing," 2024}, we estimate \$3.00 per waitlist signup and \$2.00 per post-launch signup through targeted campus events and student org partnerships
\item \textbf{***LinkedIn:} \$2.00-\$5.39 average CPC\footnote{WebFX, "How Much Does LinkedIn Advertising Cost in 2025?" webfx.com}
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Channel Selection Rationale:}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Content Marketing:} Generates 3$\times$ more leads than traditional marketing\footnote{Spendesk, "5 excellent marketing budget examples to copy," spendesk.com}
\item \textbf{Social Media:} Lower CPIs for consumer fintech at \$2.80-\$3.20 range\footnote{Business-of-Apps CPI benchmarks}
\item \textbf{Reddit:} Highly engaged finance communities with 91\% of traffic landing on conversation threads\footnote{Gupta Media, "The Reddit Advertising Playbook for 2025," guptamedia.com}
\item \textbf{University Partnerships:} Direct access to target demographic with trusted institutional backing
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Growth Strategy Assumptions}

Our acquisition strategy leverages both paid and organic channels:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Paid Acquisition:} Multi-channel focus targeting financially engaged millennials/GenZ
\item \textbf{Content-First Approach:} Building SEO foundation for long-term organic growth
\item \textbf{Community Building:} Reddit finance communities and university partnerships

\subsection{Open Banking Acceleration}

Canada's upcoming Open Banking framework presents a significant profitability catalyst. The Consumer-Driven Banking Act passed June 2024, with implementation expected early 2026\footnote{Canada.ca, "Budget 2024: Canada's Consumer-Driven Banking Framework"}.

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Cost Reduction:} 60-80\% reduction in per-user data costs\footnote{Based on UK Open Banking case studies, OBIE Annual Report 2023}
\item \textbf{Transaction Speed:} Near-instant cross-bank transfers vs. current 2-3 day EFT delays\footnote{RedCompass Labs, "Payments Modernization in Canada: Open Banking," 2025}
\item \textbf{Enhanced Data Quality:} Direct API access improves reliability
\item \textbf{Market Opportunity:} 9 million Canadians currently share credentials insecurely\footnote{Reuters, "What open banking means for Canada's saturated banking market," 2024}
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Critical Note:} As seen in the above data, Open Banking is central to our profitability and growth as a business. In addition to providing critical data and added functionality, it significantly builds on our current capabilities in relation to budgeting. For instance, the ubiquitous movement of money between accounts and institutions under current rails is either extremely expensive ("instant") or slow (EFT transfers). As such, until the implementation of Open Banking in 2026, our monthly margins will be largely unfavorable. However, as noted in the table below, only 1,200 paid users are needed to break even. We project to reach this point by month 22.
